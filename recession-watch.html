How to read the dashboard

Indicators are grouped into two rows. Headline shows three coincident variables — monthly real GDP, the unemployment rate, and CPI inflation — that summarize the current state of the economy. Leading indicators covers signals that tend to turn ahead of the broader cycle (yield curve, housing starts & building permits, motor vehicle sales, OECD US leading index), along with retail sales and the BoC Commodity Price Index for context. Recession bands shade the CD Howe Council's dated recessions; within the 2018+ window only the COVID-19 recession (Feb–Apr 2020) falls inside the frame. The colour strip on each card is a simple rule-based signal — useful as a visual shorthand, not a substitute for judgement.

Signal rules. Monthly GDP: red if negative YoY, yellow below 1%. Unemployment: red if the Sahm rule is triggered (3-month average ≥ 0.5pp above its trailing 12-month low). CPI inflation: green inside the 1–3% BoC target band, yellow outside. Yield curve: red when inverted (spread < 0). Housing starts: red if 3-month avg is >10% below 12-month avg. Vehicle sales: red if negative YoY. Retail sales: red if negative YoY, yellow below 1%. BCPI: red below −20% YoY. US CLI: red below 99 and falling; green above 100 and rising.

Data sources
